## Description

This is a fictional dataset created by IBM data scientists to explore and predict employee attrition. The dataset helps uncover the factors that lead to employee turnover and enables analysis of questions such as:
- Breakdown of distance from home by job role and attrition
- Comparison of average monthly income by education and attrition
- Patterns in work-life balance and job satisfaction

## Dataset Features

The dataset includes 35 columns with information about:
- **Demographics:** Age, Gender, Marital Status, etc.
- **Employment:** Department, Job Role, Job Level, Years at Company, etc.
- **Compensation:** Monthly Income, Hourly Rate, Salary Hike, Stock Option Level, etc.
- **Satisfaction Metrics:** Job Satisfaction, Environment Satisfaction, Relationship Satisfaction, Work-Life Balance
- **Performance:** Performance Rating, Job Involvement
- **Attrition:** Target variable indicating whether the employee left the company

## Encoding Information

- **Education:** 1='Below College', 2='College', 3='Bachelor', 4='Master', 5='Doctor'
- **EnvironmentSatisfaction:** 1='Low', 2='Medium', 3='High', 4='Very High'
- **JobInvolvement:** 1='Low', 2='Medium', 3='High', 4='Very High'
- **JobSatisfaction:** 1='Low', 2='Medium', 3='High', 4='Very High'
- **PerformanceRating:** 1='Low', 2='Good', 3='Excellent', 4='Outstanding'
- **RelationshipSatisfaction:** 1='Low', 2='Medium', 3='High', 4='Very High'
- **WorkLifeBalance:** 1='Bad', 2='Good', 3='Better', 4='Best'

## Usage

This dataset is used for educational and research purposes to build machine learning models that predict employee attrition and understand the key factors influencing employee retention.
